Everglades National Park is located in Florida. When it was created in 1947 it became the first national park to protect a fragile ecosystem.

The park is a network of wetlands and forests. It has tropical climate. Everglades National Park is home to 36 threatened and protected animal species like the Florida panther, American crocodile and West Indian manatee.

Typical of the park are cypress and mangrove trees.

Cypress trees are adapted to live in standing fresh water. They have an extensive root system to survive in the changing water level.

Mangrove trees are uniquely adapted to extreme conditions due to their high tolerance of salt water, winds, extreme tides, high temperatures, and muddy soils. The mangrove system in Everglades National Park is the largest continuous system of mangroves in the world!
