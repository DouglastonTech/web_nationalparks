Mesa Verde National Park is located in Colorado. It is a Puebloan settlement that the Puebloans built within caves and under cliffs along canyon walls. Mesa Verde is the only cultural national park in the USA.

The Puebloans lived in these cliff dwellings and the surrounding area from 600 to 1300. They build their villages using adobe. Adobe is a mixture of sand, clay, water, straw and manure! They molded this mixture into bricks and dried them in the sun.

The Puebloans didn't keep any written records. However, they left behind so many artifacts that we know a lot about their lives. They were masters in pottery and basket weaving. Mesa Verde is the largest archaeological preserve in the United States.

Mesa Verde means "green plateau" in Spanish. Spanish explorers gave the high, tree-covered plateau its name.
