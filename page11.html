Death Valley National Park is a desert. The states of California and Nevada share the park. It has been a national park since 1994.

It is the hottest place in the world with a recorded 134° F! The average temperature in July is not much less, a boiling 115° F.

It is also the driest spot in North America. The average rainfall is less than 2 inches a year. Years can go by with no rain but when it rains, there are dangerous flash floods and then a spectacular bloom of wildflowers.

It even has the lowest point in North America at 282 feet below sea level.
