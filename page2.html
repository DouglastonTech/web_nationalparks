Yellowstone National Park is the world's first national park. It was established as America's first national park in 1872 by President Ulysses S. Grant.

Do you know why?
Because the majority of the world's geysers are concentrated here!

It is located mainly in Wyoming. It got its name from the Yellowstone River that originates here.
Yellowstone National Park covers the area that is larger than the state of Rhode Island or Delaware!

Yellowstone National Park is an active super volcano. The park is hit by a few thousand earthquakes a year, there are about 10,000 thermal features, more than 300 geysers and thousands of petrified trees.

Steamboat Geyser is the largest active geyser in the world. The most well-known geyser is Old Faithful.
