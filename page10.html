Redwood National Park is located in California. It is home to the tallest trees on Earth! The tallest ones reach 380 feet!

Redwood trees are also one of the longest living organisms on our planet as their lifespan is 500-700 years with a few as old as 2000 years! Their secret of longevity is that they are highly resistant to disease. Redwood trees have a thick, protective bark and a high tannin content.

Unfortunately, too many were cut down for timber before people thought of preserving them. By the time the area received a national park status in 1968, 90% of the original redwood trees were gone.
